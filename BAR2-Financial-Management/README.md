# BAR 2: Financial Management

## ✅ What is BAR 2?

Imagine you're running a growing business and need to make big money decisions. BAR 2 is your "CFO brain" — it teaches you how to fund a business (capital structure), how much a business is worth (valuation), and whether the business is actually making money (profitability ratios).

> 🧠 **Master Mnemonic: "CLVR" (Clever)** — **C**apital structure, **L**everage, **V**aluation, **R**atios

---

## Module 1: Capital Structure

### ✅ What is Capital Structure?

Think of capital structure like a recipe — it's the mix of ingredients (debt and equity) a company uses to fund itself.

- **Debt** = Borrowing money (like a loan — you MUST pay it back with interest)
- **Equity** = Owner's money (like selling a piece of your business — no obligation to pay back)

> 🧠 **Mnemonic: "DE Mix"** — **D**ebt + **E**quity = your capital structure mix

### ✅ Cost of Capital

Every source of money has a "price tag":

| Source | Cost | ✅ Explanation |
|--------|------|------|
| Debt | Interest rate (tax-deductible) | Like paying rent on borrowed money |
| Preferred Stock | Dividend rate | Fixed dividends — like a subscription payment |
| Common Equity | Required return (CAPM) | What shareholders expect to earn |
| WACC | Weighted average | The "blended price" of all your money sources |

> 🧠 **WACC Mnemonic: "We All Count Costs"** — Weighted Average Cost of Capital

### ✅ WACC Formula (✅)

```
WACC = (Weight of Debt × Cost of Debt × (1 - Tax Rate))
     + (Weight of Preferred × Cost of Preferred)
     + (Weight of Equity × Cost of Equity)
```

Think of WACC like a smoothie — you blend different amounts of each ingredient, and the final taste is the weighted average.

### ✅ CAPM (Capital Asset Pricing Model)

```
Required Return = Risk-Free Rate + Beta × (Market Return - Risk-Free Rate)
```

> 🧠 **Mnemonic: "Risk-Free + Beta Boost"** — Start with the safe rate, then add extra return based on how risky the stock is (Beta)

- **Beta = 1** → Moves exactly with the market
- **Beta > 1** → More volatile than market (riskier)
- **Beta < 1** → Less volatile than market (safer)

### ✅ Loan Covenants

Covenants are like the rules your parents set when you borrow the car: "Don't drive over 65 mph, keep the gas tank full, don't take it on dirt roads."

- **More debt** → More stringent covenants
- **More equity** → Fewer restrictions

## Module 2: Financial Statement Analysis & Ratios

### ✅ Profitability Ratios

> 🧠 **Mnemonic: "RRRR" (4 R's of Profit)** — **R**OS, **R**OI, **R**OA, **R**OE

| Ratio | Formula | ✅ Explanation |
|-------|---------|------|
| **ROS** (Return on Sales) | Income before interest & taxes / Net Sales | How much profit per dollar of sales |
| **ROI** (Return on Investment) | Net Income / Avg Invested Capital | How well the company uses invested money |
| **ROA** (Return on Assets) | Net Income / Avg Total Assets | How well the company uses ALL its stuff |
| **ROE** (Return on Equity) | Net Income / Avg Total Equity | How well the company rewards its owners |

> 🧠 **Remember: ROE is what OWNERS care about most** — it's THEIR return

### ✅ Growth Rate

```
Growth Rate (g) = (ROA × Retention Rate) / (1 - (ROA × Retention Rate))
```

- **Retention Rate** = 1 - Dividend Payout Ratio
- The more you keep (retain), the faster you grow

> ✅ Think of it like a snowball: the more snow you keep adding (retained earnings), the bigger it gets (growth).

### ✅ Leverage

Leverage is like a seesaw — a small push on one end creates a BIG movement on the other.

| Type | ✅ Explanation | Formula |
|------|------|---------|
| **Operating Leverage** | Using fixed costs (rent, machines) instead of variable costs | High fixed costs = high operating leverage |
| **Financial Leverage** | Using debt instead of equity | More debt = more financial leverage |
| **Total Leverage** | Combined effect | Operating Leverage × Financial Leverage |

> 🧠 **Mnemonic: "OFT Leveraged"** — **O**perating, **F**inancial, **T**otal

### ✅ Degree of Operating Leverage (DOL)

```
DOL = % Change in EBIT / % Change in Sales
```

### ✅ Degree of Financial Leverage (DFL)

```
DFL = % Change in EPS / % Change in EBIT
```

### ✅ Value of a Levered Firm

```
Value of Levered Firm = Value of Unlevered Firm + PV of Interest Tax Savings
```

> ✅ Adding debt creates a "tax shield" — interest is tax deductible, so you get a tax benefit from borrowing.

## Module 3: Financial Valuation Methods

### ✅ Two Main Approaches

> 🧠 **Mnemonic: "DR" (Doctor)** — **D**iscounted Cash Flow, **R**elative Valuation

### Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) Models

Think of DCF like asking: "How much is a magic money tree worth TODAY if it will produce $100 every year for 10 years?"

| Model | ✅ Explanation |
|-------|------|
| **Dividend Discount Model (DDM)** | Values a stock based on expected future dividends |
| **Gordon Growth Model** | DDM with constant growth: Price = D₁ / (r - g) |
| **Free Cash Flow to Firm (FCFF)** | Cash left after ALL obligations |
| **Free Cash Flow to Equity (FCFE)** | Cash left for equity holders specifically |
| **Residual Income** | Income above what investors require |

> 🧠 **Gordon Growth Mnemonic: "D over R minus G"** — Dividend / (Required return - Growth)

### Relative Valuation (Price Multiples)

Instead of building a DCF, just compare to similar companies — like pricing a house by looking at what neighbors' houses sold for.

> 🧠 **Mnemonic: "PESBCF"** (Pez BCF) — **P**/E, P/**E**G, P/**S**, P/**B**, P/**CF**

| Multiple | Formula | ✅ When to Use |
|----------|---------|------|
| **P/E** (Price-to-Earnings) | P₀ / E₁ | Most widely used; earnings drive value |
| **PEG** | (P/E) / Growth Rate | When comparing companies with different growth rates |
| **P/S** (Price-to-Sales) | P₀ / S₁ | When earnings are negative; sales are harder to manipulate |
| **P/B** (Price-to-Book) | P₀ / B₀ | When book value is more stable than earnings |
| **P/CF** (Price-to-Cash-Flow) | P₀ / CF₁ | When cash flow is more reliable than earnings |

## Module 4: Financial Statement Analysis

### ✅ Liquidity Ratios

> 🧠 **Mnemonic: "CQC"** — **C**urrent ratio, **Q**uick ratio, **C**ash ratio

| Ratio | Formula | ✅ Explanation |
|-------|---------|------|
| Current Ratio | Current Assets / Current Liabilities | Can you pay bills due within a year? |
| Quick Ratio | (Cash + Receivables + Short-term Investments) / Current Liabilities | Can you pay bills RIGHT NOW? (no inventory) |
| Cash Ratio | Cash / Current Liabilities | The strictest test — cash only |

### ✅ Solvency Ratios

> 🧠 **Mnemonic: "DET"** — **D**ebt-to-equity, **E**quity multiplier, **T**imes interest earned

| Ratio | Formula | ✅ Explanation |
|-------|---------|------|
| Debt-to-Equity | Total Debt / Total Equity | How much borrowed vs. owned |
| Equity Multiplier | Total Assets / Total Equity | How leveraged the company is |
| Times Interest Earned | EBIT / Interest Expense | Can the company afford its interest payments? |

### ✅ DuPont Analysis

Breaks ROE into 3 parts to find WHERE profitability comes from:

```
ROE = Profit Margin × Asset Turnover × Equity Multiplier
ROE = (NI/Sales) × (Sales/Assets) × (Assets/Equity)
```

> 🧠 **Mnemonic: "PAE Makes ROE"** — **P**rofit margin × **A**sset turnover × **E**quity multiplier

## 🎯 Key Formulas Summary

| Formula | Expression |
|---------|------------|
| WACC | Wd × Rd × (1-T) + Wp × Rp + We × Re |
| CAPM | Rf + β(Rm - Rf) |
| Gordon Growth | P₀ = D₁ / (r - g) |
| DOL | % Δ EBIT / % Δ Sales |
| DFL | % Δ EPS / % Δ EBIT |
| DTL | DOL × DFL |
| Growth Rate | (ROA × b) / (1 - ROA × b) |
| DuPont ROE | NI/Sales × Sales/Assets × Assets/Equity |
