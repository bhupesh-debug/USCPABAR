# 📉 TBS #7: Financial Statement Analysis & Ratios

> **Frequency: ⭐⭐⭐ (Moderate-High)**
> **TBS Types: Numeric Entry, Document Review Simulation (DRS)**
> **BAR Section: BAR 2 — Financial Management + BAR 4 — Technical Reporting**

---

## 🎯 Why This is Tested on BAR TBS

Financial statement analysis combines **ratio calculation** with **analytical interpretation** — a natural fit for both Numeric Entry and DRS. The BAR exam tests this because:
- It requires application of formulas to real financial data
- DRS simulations test your ability to spot errors in analytical memos
- It connects financial management (BAR 2) with reporting (BAR 4)
- The AICPA Blueprint explicitly includes "analytical procedures" and "financial analysis"

### ✅ The Big Picture
Financial statement analysis is about turning raw numbers into **meaningful insights**. You calculate ratios, compare them across periods and competitors, and draw conclusions about a company's health.

## 📊 The Five Categories of Ratios

### ✅ Mnemonic: **"LAMPS"** — The 5 Pillars of Financial Analysis

| Letter | Category | What It Measures |
|--------|----------|-----------------|
| **L**iquidity | Short-term ability to pay debts | Can you pay bills due NOW? |
| **A**ctivity (Efficiency) | How well assets are used | Are you using resources wisely? |
| **M**arket (Valuation) | Investor perception | What do investors think? |
| **P**rofitability | Earnings generation | Are you making money? |
| **S**olvency (Leverage) | Long-term debt capacity | Can you survive long-term? |

---

## 💧 Liquidity Ratios

### ✅ Mnemonic: **"CQC-WW"** = Current, Quick, Cash, Working Capital

| Ratio | Formula | Benchmark |
|-------|---------|-----------|
| **Current Ratio** | Current Assets ÷ Current Liabilities | > 2.0 is strong |
| **Quick Ratio** (Acid Test) | (Cash + Short-term Investments + Net Receivables) ÷ Current Liabilities | > 1.0 is good |
| **Cash Ratio** | (Cash + Short-term Investments) ÷ Current Liabilities | Most conservative |
| **Working Capital** | Current Assets − Current Liabilities | Positive = good |

### ✅ Key Insight: Quick Ratio EXCLUDES inventory and prepaid expenses
- Why? Inventory may not sell quickly; prepaids can't pay bills
- Quick = "What if we had to pay all current debts RIGHT NOW with liquid assets?"

### ✅ Mnemonic: **"Quick drops the SLOW stuff"** (inventory = slow to convert)

---

## ⚡ Activity (Efficiency) Ratios

### ✅ Mnemonic: **"TRIP"** = Turnover for Receivables, Inventory, Payables

| Ratio | Formula | What It Tells You |
|-------|---------|-------------------|
| **Receivables Turnover** | Net Credit Sales ÷ Avg Accounts Receivable | How fast you collect |
| **Days Sales Outstanding** | 365 ÷ Receivables Turnover | Average collection period |
| **Inventory Turnover** | COGS ÷ Average Inventory | How fast inventory sells |
| **Days in Inventory** | 365 ÷ Inventory Turnover | How long inventory sits |
| **Payables Turnover** | COGS (or Purchases) ÷ Avg Accounts Payable | How fast you pay suppliers |
| **Days Payable Outstanding** | 365 ÷ Payables Turnover | Average payment period |
| **Total Asset Turnover** | Net Sales ÷ Average Total Assets | Revenue per dollar of assets |
| **Fixed Asset Turnover** | Net Sales ÷ Average Net Fixed Assets | Revenue per dollar of PP&E |

### The Cash Conversion Cycle (CCC):
```
CCC = Days in Inventory + Days Sales Outstanding − Days Payable Outstanding
```

### ✅ Key Insight: LOWER CCC = BETTER (you convert cash faster)
- High CCC → cash is "trapped" in operations
- Negative CCC → you collect from customers before paying suppliers (rare, but Amazon does this!)

## 💰 Profitability Ratios

### ✅ Mnemonic: **"GROAN-RE"** = Gross, Return on Assets, Net, Return on Equity

| Ratio | Formula | What It Tells You |
|-------|---------|-------------------|
| **Gross Profit Margin** | (Sales − COGS) ÷ Sales | Profitability before operating costs |
| **Operating Profit Margin** | Operating Income ÷ Sales | Profitability from core operations |
| **Net Profit Margin** | Net Income ÷ Sales | Bottom-line profitability |
| **Return on Assets (ROA)** | Net Income ÷ Average Total Assets | How well assets generate profit |
| **Return on Equity (ROE)** | Net Income ÷ Average Stockholders' Equity | Return to shareholders |
| **Earnings Per Share (EPS)** | (Net Income − Preferred Dividends) ÷ Weighted Avg Common Shares | Per-share earnings |

### The DuPont Analysis — Decomposing ROE

```
ROE = Net Profit Margin × Asset Turnover × Equity Multiplier
    = (Net Income/Sales) × (Sales/Assets) × (Assets/Equity)
```

### ✅ Mnemonic: **"DuPont = MAT"** = Margin × Activity × Turn (leverage)

| Component | Measures | How to Improve |
|-----------|----------|----------------|
| Net Profit Margin | Profitability | Cut costs, increase prices |
| Asset Turnover | Efficiency | Generate more sales per asset dollar |
| Equity Multiplier | Leverage | Use more debt (⚠️ increases risk) |

### Extended DuPont (5-Factor):
```
ROE = Tax Burden × Interest Burden × EBIT Margin × Asset Turnover × Equity Multiplier
    = (NI/EBT) × (EBT/EBIT) × (EBIT/Sales) × (Sales/Assets) × (Assets/Equity)
```

## 🏦 Solvency (Leverage) Ratios

### ✅ Mnemonic: **"DIET"** = Debt-to-Equity, Interest coverage, Equity ratio, Total debt

| Ratio | Formula | What It Tells You |
|-------|---------|-------------------|
| **Debt-to-Equity** | Total Liabilities ÷ Total Equity | Leverage level |
| **Debt-to-Assets (Debt Ratio)** | Total Liabilities ÷ Total Assets | % of assets funded by debt |
| **Equity Ratio** | Total Equity ÷ Total Assets | % of assets funded by equity |
| **Interest Coverage (TIE)** | EBIT ÷ Interest Expense | Ability to pay interest |
| **Debt Service Coverage** | (Net Income + Depreciation + Interest) ÷ (Principal + Interest) | Ability to service all debt |
| **Equity Multiplier** | Total Assets ÷ Total Equity | Leverage multiplier (DuPont) |

### ✅ Key Insight:
- Debt-to-Equity + Interest Coverage are the TWO most asked ratios
- Higher D/E = more leverage = more risk = potentially higher ROE
- Interest Coverage < 1.5 = danger zone (may not cover interest!)

---

## 📈 Market (Valuation) Ratios

| Ratio | Formula | What It Tells You |
|-------|---------|-------------------|
| **Price-to-Earnings (P/E)** | Market Price per Share ÷ EPS | What investors pay per $1 of earnings |
| **Price-to-Book (P/B)** | Market Price per Share ÷ Book Value per Share | Premium over accounting value |
| **Dividend Yield** | Dividends per Share ÷ Market Price per Share | Cash return to investors |
| **Dividend Payout Ratio** | Dividends ÷ Net Income | % of earnings paid as dividends |
| **Book Value per Share** | (Total Equity − Preferred Equity) ÷ Common Shares Outstanding | Accounting value per share |

## 🔥 TBS Walkthrough: Complete Ratio Analysis

### Scenario:
**AnalyzeCo provides the following financial data:**

**Income Statement (Year 1):**
| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Net Sales | $5,000,000 |
| Cost of Goods Sold | $3,000,000 |
| Operating Expenses | $1,200,000 |
| Interest Expense | $200,000 |
| Tax Expense | $150,000 |
| Net Income | $450,000 |

**Balance Sheet (Year 1 Ending):**
| Item | Amount |
|------|--------|
| Cash | $300,000 |
| Accounts Receivable | $600,000 |
| Inventory | $800,000 |
| Total Current Assets | $1,700,000 |
| Net Fixed Assets | $3,300,000 |
| Total Assets | $5,000,000 |
| Current Liabilities | $900,000 |
| Long-term Debt | $1,600,000 |
| Total Liabilities | $2,500,000 |
| Total Equity | $2,500,000 |

**Additional:**
- Average Total Assets: $4,800,000
- Average Equity: $2,400,000
- Average A/R: $550,000
- Average Inventory: $750,000
- Common shares: 100,000
- Market price: $30/share
- Dividends paid: $100,000

### Calculated Ratios:

**Liquidity:**
```
Current Ratio = $1,700,000 / $900,000 = 1.89
Quick Ratio = ($300,000 + $600,000) / $900,000 = 1.00
Working Capital = $1,700,000 − $900,000 = $800,000
```

**Activity:**
```
Receivables Turnover = $5,000,000 / $550,000 = 9.09
Days Sales Outstanding = 365 / 9.09 = 40.2 days
Inventory Turnover = $3,000,000 / $750,000 = 4.00
Days in Inventory = 365 / 4.00 = 91.3 days
Total Asset Turnover = $5,000,000 / $4,800,000 = 1.04
```

**Profitability:**
```
Gross Profit Margin = ($5,000,000 − $3,000,000) / $5,000,000 = 40.0%
Operating Profit Margin = ($5,000,000 − $3,000,000 − $1,200,000) / $5,000,000 = 16.0%
Net Profit Margin = $450,000 / $5,000,000 = 9.0%
ROA = $450,000 / $4,800,000 = 9.4%
ROE = $450,000 / $2,400,000 = 18.75%
EPS = $450,000 / 100,000 = $4.50
```

**DuPont Analysis:**
```
ROE = Net Margin × Asset Turnover × Equity Multiplier
    = 9.0% × 1.04 × ($5,000,000 / $2,500,000)
    = 9.0% × 1.04 × 2.0
    = 18.72% ≈ 18.75% (rounding)
```

**Solvency:**
```
Debt-to-Equity = $2,500,000 / $2,500,000 = 1.00
Debt Ratio = $2,500,000 / $5,000,000 = 50%
Interest Coverage = $800,000 / $200,000 = 4.0x
(EBIT = $5M − $3M − $1.2M = $800K)
```

**Market:**
```
P/E Ratio = $30 / $4.50 = 6.67
Dividend Yield = ($100,000/100,000) / $30 = $1.00 / $30 = 3.33%
Dividend Payout = $100,000 / $450,000 = 22.2%
```

## 🔗 Strategic Frameworks from Blog Resources

### Porter's Five Forces (from [uscpabar.blogspot.com](https://uscpabar.blogspot.com))
Applied in TBS when analyzing industry profitability or competitive position:

| Force | Analysis Question |
|-------|-------------------|
| Threat of New Entrants | How easy is it for competitors to enter? |
| Bargaining Power of Suppliers | Can suppliers dictate terms? |
| Bargaining Power of Buyers | Can customers force lower prices? |
| Threat of Substitutes | Are there alternative products? |
| Competitive Rivalry | How intense is current competition? |

### ✅ Mnemonic: **"BREST"** = Buyers, Rivalry, Entry, Substitutes, (supplier) Threats

### Blue Ocean Strategy (from [uscpabar.blogspot.com](https://uscpabar.blogspot.com))
- **Red Ocean** = Compete in existing market (fight for market share)
- **Blue Ocean** = Create uncontested market space (make competition irrelevant)
- **Value Innovation** = Simultaneously pursue differentiation AND low cost
- Framework: **ERRC Grid** — Eliminate, Reduce, Raise, Create

### Value-Risk Flywheel (from [uscpabar.blogspot.com](https://uscpabar.blogspot.com))
- Every business decision creates BOTH value and risk
- Risk management should be integrated with value creation
- Connects to COSO ERM framework (BAR 1)
- On TBS: May appear in DRS where you analyze a strategic memo for risk/value trade-offs

## ⚡ Common TBS Traps & How to Avoid Them

| Trap | What Students Do Wrong | Correct Treatment |
|------|----------------------|-------------------|
| Avg vs. ending values | Use ending balance sheet numbers | Use **AVERAGE** for turnover ratios (avg of beginning + ending) |
| Quick ratio inventory | Include inventory | Quick ratio **EXCLUDES** inventory and prepaids |
| EBIT for interest coverage | Use net income | Use **EBIT** (earnings before interest and taxes) |
| ROA denominator | Use ending total assets | Use **Average** total assets |
| Receivables turnover | Use total sales | Use **Net Credit Sales** (or net sales if not specified) |
| Inventory turnover | Use sales | Use **COGS**, not sales |
| DuPont equity multiplier | Use D/E ratio | Equity Multiplier = **Assets/Equity**, NOT D/E |
| Trend analysis direction | Higher is always better | Depends on the ratio (higher D/E = more risk!) |

## 📝 Practice Scenario — Try This!

**RatioCo provides two years of data:**

| Item | Year 2 | Year 1 |
|------|--------|--------|
| Net Sales | $8,000,000 | $6,500,000 |
| COGS | $5,200,000 | $4,200,000 |
| Net Income | $560,000 | $460,000 |
| EBIT | $1,000,000 | $800,000 |
| Interest Expense | $200,000 | $150,000 |
| Total Assets (ending) | $6,000,000 | $5,000,000 |
| Total Equity (ending) | $3,000,000 | $2,500,000 |
| Current Assets | $2,000,000 | $1,600,000 |
| Inventory | $900,000 | $700,000 |
| Current Liabilities | $1,200,000 | $900,000 |

**Calculate for Year 2:**
1. Current Ratio
2. Gross Profit Margin
3. ROA
4. Debt-to-Equity
5. Interest Coverage
6. DuPont ROE

<details>
<summary>💡 Click for Solution</summary>

**1. Current Ratio:**
```
= $2,000,000 / $1,200,000 = 1.67
```

**2. Gross Profit Margin:**
```
= ($8,000,000 − $5,200,000) / $8,000,000 = 35.0%
```

**3. ROA:**
```
Average Assets = ($6,000,000 + $5,000,000) / 2 = $5,500,000
ROA = $560,000 / $5,500,000 = 10.18%
```

**4. Debt-to-Equity:**
```
Total Liabilities = $6,000,000 − $3,000,000 = $3,000,000
D/E = $3,000,000 / $3,000,000 = 1.00
```

**5. Interest Coverage:**
```
= $1,000,000 / $200,000 = 5.0x
```

**6. DuPont ROE:**
```
Net Margin = $560,000 / $8,000,000 = 7.0%
Asset Turnover = $8,000,000 / $5,500,000 = 1.4545
Equity Multiplier = $6,000,000 / $3,000,000 = 2.0
ROE = 7.0% × 1.4545 × 2.0 = 20.36%

Verify: $560,000 / Avg Equity = $560,000 / $2,750,000 = 20.36% ✓
```

</details>

> 💡 **Top Ranker Tip**: On DRS simulations, they'll give you a financial analysis memo with errors. The most common errors are: (1) using ending balances instead of averages, (2) using net income instead of EBIT for interest coverage, and (3) including inventory in the quick ratio. Scan for these THREE errors first — they appear on almost every DRS.
