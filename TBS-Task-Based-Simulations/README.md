## 🎯 What Are Task-Based Simulations?

Task-Based Simulations (TBS) are **mini case studies** that test your ability to apply accounting knowledge to real-world scenarios. Unlike MCQs (which test recognition), TBS tests **application and analysis** — you must actually DO the accounting.

### ✅ Think of TBS Like This:
- MCQs = Multiple choice quiz (pick the right answer)
- TBS = Open-book practical exam (show your work, apply the rules, analyze the data)

## 🔥 Types of TBS You'll Encounter

### 1. Option List TBS
- Drop-down menus to select the correct answer from a list
- Common for: Account classifications, disclosure requirements, fund types

### 2. Numeric Entry TBS
- You type in a calculated number (no answer choices!)
- Common for: Variance analysis, ratio calculations, lease payments, consolidation entries
- ⚠️ **Watch out for rounding instructions!**

### 3. Journal Entry TBS
- Select accounts and enter debits/credits
- Common for: Business combinations, hedge accounting, lease entries
- ✅ **KEY**: Know your debits and credits COLD

### 4. Document Review Simulation (DRS)
- Review memos, emails, financial statements for errors
- Click on incorrect items and select the correct treatment
- Common for: Financial statement analysis, disclosure reviews, government reports
- ✅ **This is the HARDEST type** — requires deep understanding

### ✅ Mnemonic for TBS Types: **"O-N-J-D"** = **"Only Ninjas Jump Deftly"**
- **O**ption List
- **N**umeric Entry
- **J**ournal Entry
- **D**ocument Review Simulation

## 🧠 TBS Strategy — How Top Rankers Approach Simulations

### The 3-Pass Method (Used by Top 1% Scorers)

**Pass 1: SCAN (2 minutes)**
- Read the entire simulation quickly
- Identify the TBS type (Journal Entry? DRS? Numeric?)
- Note what data/exhibits are provided
- Understand what they're ASKING for

**Pass 2: SOLVE (8-12 minutes)**
- Work through the requirements systematically
- Use the exhibits/documents provided
- Show calculations on scratch paper (even if not submitted)
- Enter your answers carefully

**Pass 3: VERIFY (2 minutes)**
- Double-check your entries
- Verify debits = credits (for journal entries)
- Check that numbers make sense (reasonableness test)
- Make sure you answered EVERY part

### ✅ Mnemonic: **"S-S-V"** = **"Scan, Solve, Verify"**

## ⚡ TBS Power Tips from Top Rankers

### 1. Partial Credit is Your Best Friend
- Unlike MCQs (all-or-nothing), TBS gives **partial credit**
- Even if you're unsure, ALWAYS attempt every part
- A half-right answer > a blank answer

### 2. Time Management is Everything
- You get ~225 minutes total for the BAR exam
- Budget ~15 minutes per TBS (105 minutes total for 7 TBS)
- Leave ~120 minutes for 66 MCQs (~1.8 min each)
- **Don't spend 30 minutes on one TBS** — move on and come back

### 3. Know Your Authoritative Literature
- TBS may reference ASC topics — know the big ones:
  - ASC 606 (Revenue), ASC 842 (Leases), ASC 805 (Combinations)
  - ASC 815 (Derivatives), ASC 820 (Fair Value)
- You can search the authoritative literature during the exam!

### 4. Practice the Software
- AICPA offers a free **Sample Test** at [aicpa.org](https://www.aicpa.org)
- Get comfortable with the spreadsheet tool, drop-downs, and navigation
- The interface is different from Becker/Wiley practice software

### 5. Read EVERY Exhibit
- TBS often include 3-5 tabs of information
- The answer is usually hidden in the exhibits
- **Don't skip any tab** — they're all there for a reason
