# BAR 5: State & Local Governments

## ✅ What is BAR 5?

Government accounting is a COMPLETELY different world from business accounting. Businesses care about PROFIT. Governments care about: "Did we spend taxpayer money the way we said we would?"

Think of it this way: A business is like a restaurant (goal = make money). A government is like a school cafeteria (goal = feed everyone within the budget).

> 🧠 **Master Mnemonic: "FANG B"** — **F**und types, **A**nnual comprehensive financial report, **N**et position, **G**overnment-wide statements, **B**udgetary accounting

---

## ✅ The Two Sets of Financial Statements

Governments prepare TWO different views of their finances (this is unique to government):

| View | Measurement Focus | Basis | ✅ Explanation |
|------|-------------------|-------|------|
| **Government-Wide** | Economic resources | Full accrual | Like a business — shows ALL resources and obligations |
| **Fund-Level** | Current financial resources (for governmental funds) | Modified accrual | Like a checkbook — shows what's available to spend NOW |

> 🧠 **Mnemonic: "Wide = Whole picture, Fund = Focused view"**

---

## ✅ Three Types of Funds

> 🧠 **Mnemonic: "GPF"** — **G**overnmental, **P**roprietary, **F**iduciary

### 1. Governmental Funds (The Main Ones)

> 🧠 **Mnemonic: "GRaDS CP"** — **G**eneral, **R**evenue (special), **D**ebt **S**ervice, **C**apital projects, **P**ermanent

| Fund | ✅ Explanation |
|------|------|
| **General Fund** | The "main checking account" — catches everything that doesn't go elsewhere |
| **Special Revenue** | Money earmarked for specific purposes (like a gas tax for road repairs) |
| **Debt Service** | Money set aside to pay off long-term debt (principal + interest) |
| **Capital Projects** | Money for building big things (new school, bridge, park) |
| **Permanent** | Endowments — spend the earnings, but keep the principal forever |

> ✅ Think of the General Fund like a catch-all junk drawer — if a transaction doesn't belong somewhere specific, it goes here.

### 2. Proprietary Funds (Business-Like)

> 🧠 **Mnemonic: "EI"** — **E**nterprise, **I**nternal service

| Fund | ✅ Explanation |
|------|------|
| **Enterprise Fund** | Government activities that charge the public (water utility, parking garage, airport) |
| **Internal Service Fund** | Government activities that serve other government departments (IT services, motor pool) |

> ✅ Enterprise = serves EXTERNAL users (the public). Internal Service = serves INTERNAL users (other departments).

### 3. Fiduciary Funds (Holding Money for Others)

> 🧠 **Mnemonic: "PICA"** — **P**ension trust, **I**nvestment trust, **C**ustodial, **A**gent (private-purpose trust)

| Fund | ✅ Explanation |
|------|------|
| **Pension Trust** | Money held for employee retirement benefits |
| **Investment Trust** | Money invested on behalf of external participants |
| **Private-Purpose Trust** | Money held in trust where BOTH principal and earnings benefit others |
| **Custodial** | Money temporarily held for others (like collecting taxes for another government) |

## ✅ Measurement Focus & Basis of Accounting

This is the #1 most tested concept in government accounting:

| Fund Type | Measurement Focus | Basis of Accounting | ✅ Think of it as... |
|-----------|-------------------|---------------------|------|
| **Governmental Funds** | Current financial resources | Modified accrual | "What can I spend NOW?" |
| **Proprietary Funds** | Economic resources | Full accrual | "What's the full picture?" (like a business) |
| **Fiduciary Funds** | Economic resources | Full accrual | "What's the full picture?" (like a business) |
| **Government-Wide** | Economic resources | Full accrual | "What's the full picture?" (like a business) |

> 🧠 **Mnemonic: "Only Governmental funds use Modified accrual — everyone else uses Full accrual"**

### Modified Accrual Rules

- **Revenue:** Recognized when MEASURABLE and AVAILABLE (collected within 60 days of year-end)
- **Expenditures:** Recognized when the liability is incurred (not when cash is paid)

> 🧠 **Mnemonic: "MA Revenue = Measurable + Available"**

## ✅ Government-Wide Financial Statements

| Statement | What It Shows |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Statement of Net Position** | Like a balance sheet — Assets + Deferred Outflows - Liabilities - Deferred Inflows = Net Position |
| **Statement of Activities** | Like an income statement — shows program revenues vs. expenses and general revenues |

### ✅ Net Position Categories

> 🧠 **Mnemonic: "NUR"** — **N**et investment in capital assets, **U**nrestricted, **R**estricted

| Category | ✅ Explanation |
|----------|------|
| **Net Investment in Capital Assets** | Value of roads, buildings, etc. minus related debt |
| **Restricted** | Money that can only be used for specific purposes (by law or donors) |
| **Unrestricted** | Money the government can use for anything |

---

## ✅ Annual Comprehensive Financial Report (ACFR)

The ACFR is the government's "annual report" — like a company's 10-K but for government.

> 🧠 **Mnemonic: "ISS"** — **I**ntroductory section, **S**tatistical section, and the middle is the most important: **Financial section**

### Financial Section Contents

| Component | ✅ Explanation |
|-----------|------|
| **MD&A** (Management's Discussion & Analysis) | RSI — management explains the numbers in plain English |
| **Government-Wide Statements** | Full accrual — the big picture |
| **Fund Financial Statements** | Modified accrual for governmental; full accrual for proprietary/fiduciary |
| **Notes to Financial Statements** | Essential details that explain the numbers |
| **RSI** (Required Supplementary Information) | Extra info required by GASB (pension schedules, budgetary comparisons) |

## ✅ Budgetary Accounting

### ✅ Why is it special?

Governments are LEGALLY required to follow their budgets. A business can overspend — a government might break the law if it does.

### ✅ Budgetary Journal Entry (Beginning of Year)

```
DR  Estimated Revenues           1,000,000
    CR  Appropriations                        950,000
    CR  Budgetary Fund Balance                 50,000
```

> ✅ This entry records the government's "game plan" — how much they expect to collect (estimated revenues) and how much they're allowed to spend (appropriations).

### ✅ Encumbrances

> ✅ An encumbrance is like putting a "hold" on your budget — you haven't spent the money yet, but you've committed to spending it (like placing a purchase order).

```
DR  Encumbrances              XXX
    CR  Budgetary Fund Balance Reserved     XXX
```

> 🧠 **Mnemonic: "Encumbrance = Earmarked but not yet spent"**

## ✅ Key Topics Frequently Tested

### Capital Assets in Government

- Governments report infrastructure (roads, bridges, sewers)
- Can use either **depreciation** or **modified approach** (maintain condition level)
- Art and historical treasures can be exempted from capitalization

### Interfund Activity

> 🧠 **Mnemonic: "TROLL"** — **T**ransfers, **R**eimbursements, **O**utstanding loans, **L**oans, **L**ong-term

| Type | ✅ Explanation |
|------|------|
| **Transfers** | Moving money between funds — NOT revenues or expenditures |
| **Reimbursements** | One fund repays another for costs initially recorded in the wrong fund |
| **Loans** | One fund lends to another — recorded as receivable/payable |

### Nonexchange Revenue Transactions

> 🧠 **Mnemonic: "DIGS"** — **D**erived tax revenue (sales/income tax), **I**mposed nonexchange (property tax, fines), **G**overnment-mandated nonexchange (federal grants with requirements), **S**haring (voluntary nonexchange like donations)

## 🎯 Key Formulas Summary

| Formula | Expression |
|---------|------------|
| Net Position | Assets + Deferred Outflows - Liabilities - Deferred Inflows |
| Modified Accrual Revenue | Measurable AND Available (within 60 days) |
| Budgetary Entry | DR Est. Revenues, CR Appropriations, CR/DR Fund Balance |
| Encumbrance Entry | DR Encumbrances, CR Fund Balance Reserved |
