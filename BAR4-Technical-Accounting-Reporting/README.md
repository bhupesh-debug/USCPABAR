# BAR 4: Technical Accounting & Reporting

## ✅ What is BAR 4?

BAR 4 is like the "advanced rulebook" of accounting. If FAR gave you the basics, BAR 4 takes you deeper into the complex transactions that big companies deal with — mergers, derivatives, complex leases, and more.

> 🧠 **Master Mnemonic: "REBELS DC"** — **R**evenue recognition, **E**mployee benefits, **B**usiness combinations, **E**quity (stock) compensation, **L**eases, **S**oftware, **D**erivatives, **C**onsolidations

---

## Module 1: Indefinite-Lived Intangible Assets & Goodwill

### ✅ What is Goodwill?

Imagine buying a pizza shop. The ingredients and ovens are worth $100K, but you pay $150K because the shop has loyal customers and a great reputation. That extra $50K? That's goodwill.

```
Goodwill = Purchase Price - Fair Value of Net Identifiable Assets
```

### ✅ Intangible Asset Types

| Type | Amortize? | Impairment Test | ✅ Explanation |
|------|-----------|-----------------|------|
| **Finite Life** | Yes — over useful life | Two-step test | Like a patent that expires in 20 years |
| **Indefinite Life** | No | One-step test | Like a trademark that lasts forever |
| **Goodwill** | No (public) / Optional (private) | Impairment test | The premium paid above fair value in an acquisition |

> 🧠 **Mnemonic: "FIG"** — **F**inite (amortize), **I**ndefinite (no amortize), **G**oodwill (impairment only)

### ✅ Goodwill Impairment

> ✅ Think of impairment like a car losing value after an accident — you have to "write it down" to its new lower value.

**Public Companies:** Compare fair value of reporting unit to carrying amount. If FV < carrying amount, impairment = the difference (but limited to the amount of goodwill).

## Module 2: Revenue Recognition (ASC 606)

### ✅ The 5-Step Model

> 🧠 **Mnemonic: "I DART"** — **I**dentify contract, **D**etermine transaction price, **A**llocate price, **R**ecognize revenue, **T**rade (identify performance obligations)

Wait — better order:

> 🧠 **Official 5 Steps: "CIDAR"** — **C**ontract identified, **I**dentify performance obligations, **D**etermine transaction price, **A**llocate price, **R**ecognize revenue

| Step | Action | ✅ Explanation |
|------|--------|------|
| 1 | Identify the contract | Is there a real agreement? Both parties agreed? |
| 2 | Identify performance obligations | What do you promise to deliver? Each distinct promise = separate obligation |
| 3 | Determine transaction price | How much will you get paid? Consider discounts, variable consideration |
| 4 | Allocate price to obligations | If multiple promises, split the price among them |
| 5 | Recognize revenue | Record revenue when (or as) you deliver on each promise |

### ✅ Over Time vs. Point in Time

| When | ✅ Explanation |
|------|------|
| **Over Time** | Customer receives benefits as you work (construction contracts, cleaning services) |
| **Point in Time** | Customer gets the good/service at one moment (selling a car, delivering a product) |

> 🧠 **Mnemonic: "CON" for over time** — **C**ustomer controls, **O**ngoing benefit, **N**o alternative use + right to payment

## Module 3: Stock Compensation (Share-Based Payments)

### ✅ What is it?

Companies pay employees with stock options instead of (or in addition to) cash. Think of it as "You can buy our stock at a discount in the future."

### ✅ Key Concepts

| Term | ✅ Explanation |
|------|------|
| **Grant Date** | The day the company gives you the option |
| **Vesting Period** | How long you must wait before you can use it (like a waiting period) |
| **Exercise Price** | The price you'll pay to buy the stock |
| **Fair Value** | What the option is worth on grant date (usually Black-Scholes model) |

> 🧠 **Mnemonic: "GVEF"** — **G**rant, **V**esting, **E**xercise, **F**air value

**Expense Recognition:** Total compensation cost = Fair value on grant date × Number of options. Spread this expense over the vesting period.

## Module 4: Business Combinations & Consolidations

### ✅ Acquisition Method

When one company buys another, the buyer uses the **Acquisition Method**:

> 🧠 **Mnemonic: "BIG FACE"** — **B**uyer identified, **I**dentify acquisition date, **G**oodwill calculated, **F**air value measured, **A**ssets/liabilities at FV, **C**onsideration transferred, **E**liminate intercompany

### ✅ Consolidation Steps

1. **Adjust subsidiary assets/liabilities to fair value**
2. **Identify any identifiable intangible assets**
3. **Calculate goodwill** (or bargain purchase gain)
4. **Eliminate intercompany transactions**
5. **Record noncontrolling interest** (if less than 100% acquired)

### ✅ Noncontrolling Interest (NCI)

> ✅ If you buy 70% of a company, the other 30% belongs to minority shareholders — that's the NCI.

```
NCI on Balance Sheet = FV of subsidiary × NCI %
NCI Income = Subsidiary Net Income × NCI %
```

### ✅ Eliminating Entry ("CAR BIG")

> 🧠 **Mnemonic: "CAR BIG"**

| Debit (Eliminate) | What It Means |
|-------------------|---------------|
| **C**ommon stock (subsidiary) | Remove the sub's equity |
| **A**PIC (subsidiary) | Remove the sub's additional paid-in capital |
| **R**etained earnings (subsidiary) | Remove the sub's retained earnings |
| **B**alance sheet adjustments to FV | Adjust to fair value |
| **I**dentifiable intangibles | Record any new intangible assets |
| **G**oodwill | Record excess purchase price |

Credit: Investment in Subsidiary + NCI

## Module 5: Derivatives & Hedge Accounting

### ✅ What are Derivatives?

> ✅ A derivative is like an insurance policy for financial risk. Its value is "derived" from something else (stock price, interest rate, currency rate).

| Type | ✅ Explanation | Example |
|------|------|---------|
| **Forward** | Agreement to buy/sell at a future date at a set price | Locking in the price of wheat for delivery in 6 months |
| **Future** | Same as forward but standardized and traded on exchanges | Buying an oil futures contract on the NYMEX |
| **Option** | RIGHT (not obligation) to buy/sell at a set price | Buying a call option on Apple stock |
| **Swap** | Exchanging one stream of payments for another | Swapping a variable interest rate for a fixed rate |

> 🧠 **Mnemonic: "FFOS"** — **F**orward, **F**utures, **O**ptions, **S**waps

### ✅ Hedge Types

| Hedge Type | Purpose | Where Gains/Losses Go |
|------------|---------|----------------------|
| **Fair Value Hedge** | Protects against changes in fair value of an asset/liability | Income statement (currently) |
| **Cash Flow Hedge** | Protects against variability in future cash flows | OCI first → reclassified to income when transaction occurs |
| **Net Investment Hedge** | Protects against foreign currency risk in subsidiaries | OCI (CTA) |

> 🧠 **Mnemonic: "FaCeN"** — **Fa**ir value → income, **C**ash flow → OCI first, **N**et investment → OCI

## Module 6: Leases (ASC 842)

### ✅ Lessee Classification

| Type | ✅ Explanation | Balance Sheet | Income Statement |
|------|------|---------------|------------------|
| **Finance Lease** | Basically buying the asset on installments | ROU Asset + Lease Liability | Amortization + Interest (front-loaded) |
| **Operating Lease** | Renting — you give it back | ROU Asset + Lease Liability | Single straight-line lease expense |

> 🧠 **Mnemonic: "OWNS IT" = Finance Lease** — if any of these are true, it's a finance lease:

| Test | Criteria |
|------|----------|
| **O**wnership transfer | Title passes to lessee at end |
| **W**ritten purchase option | Lessee is reasonably certain to exercise |
| **N**early all economic life | Lease term ≥ 75% of useful life |
| **S**ubstantially all fair value | PV of payments ≥ 90% of FV |
| **I**mpractical alternative use | Asset is so specialized only the lessee can use it |
| **T**ransfer (one of the above) | Any ONE makes it a finance lease |

## Module 7: Public Company Reporting Topics

### ✅ Key Topics

| Topic | ✅ Explanation |
|-------|------|
| **Segment Reporting** | Breaking financial results down by business segment (like "North America" vs "Europe") |
| **Interim Reporting** | Quarterly financial statements (Q1, Q2, Q3) — treated as integral part of the annual period |
| **Earnings Per Share** | Basic EPS = (NI - Preferred Dividends) / Weighted Avg Shares Outstanding |
| **Subsequent Events** | Things that happen AFTER the balance sheet date but BEFORE the statements are issued |

---

## 🎯 Key Formulas Summary

| Formula | Expression |
|---------|------------|
| Goodwill | FV of subsidiary - FV of net identifiable assets |
| NCI Balance Sheet | FV of subsidiary × NCI% |
| Basic EPS | (NI - Pref Div) / Weighted Avg Shares |
| Diluted EPS | Adjusted NI / (Weighted Avg Shares + Dilutive Securities) |
| Finance Lease Liability | PV of lease payments |
