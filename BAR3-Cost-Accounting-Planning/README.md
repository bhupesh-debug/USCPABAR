# BAR 3: Cost Accounting & Planning

## ✅ What is BAR 3?

Imagine you run a cookie factory. BAR 3 answers: "How much does each cookie cost?" (cost accounting), "Did we spend more flour than planned?" (variance analysis), "How many cookies should we bake next month?" (budgeting), and "What will cookie sales look like next year?" (forecasting).

> 🧠 **Master Mnemonic: "CVBF" — Counting Very Big Figures** — **C**ost accounting, **V**ariance analysis, **B**udgeting, **F**orecasting

---

## Module 1: Cost Accounting

### ✅ Cost Classifications

> 🧠 **Mnemonic: "DIM" costs** — **D**irect Materials, **I**ndirect (Overhead), **M**anpower (Direct Labor)

| Cost Type | ✅ Explanation | Example |
|-----------|------|---------|
| **Direct Materials** | Raw stuff you can trace to the product | Flour in cookies |
| **Direct Labor** | Workers directly making the product | The baker |
| **Manufacturing Overhead** | Everything else in the factory | Electricity, rent, supervisor salary |

### ✅ Product vs. Period Costs

| Type | ✅ Explanation | Where It Goes |
|------|------|---------------|
| **Product Costs** | Costs to MAKE the product (DM + DL + OH) | Inventory → COGS when sold |
| **Period Costs** | Costs to RUN the business (selling, admin) | Expense immediately on income statement |

> 🧠 **Remember: "Product goes to inventory, Period goes to expense"**

### ✅ Job-Order vs. Process Costing

| Method | ✅ Explanation | Best For |
|--------|------|----------|
| **Job-Order** | Tracks costs per individual job/batch | Custom work (construction, printing, aircraft) |
| **Process** | Averages costs across many identical units | Mass production (paint, chemicals, cereal) |

> 🧠 **Mnemonic: "J = Jumbo unique jobs, P = Piles of identical products"**

### ✅ Process Costing Steps

> 🧠 **Mnemonic: "SCACA"** — **S**ummarize physical units, **C**alculate equivalent units, **A**ccumulate total costs, **C**alculate unit costs, **A**pply costs

### ✅ Equivalent Units

Think of equivalent units like this: If you have 100 cookies that are 50% baked, that equals 50 FULLY baked cookies in terms of work done.

**Two Methods:**
- **FIFO**: Only counts work done THIS period
- **Weighted Average**: Blends beginning inventory + current work

### ✅ Absorption vs. Variable Costing

| Method | Fixed OH Treatment | ✅ Explanation |
|--------|-------------------|------|
| **Absorption** | Included in product cost | Required by GAAP — ALL factory costs go into inventory |
| **Variable** | Period expense | Only for internal use — only variable costs go into inventory |

> 🧠 **Mnemonic: "Absorption Absorbs ALL"** (including fixed overhead into product costs)

**Key Rule:** When production > sales → Absorption income > Variable income (because fixed OH is "hiding" in inventory)

## Module 2: Variance Analysis

### ✅ What is Variance Analysis?

It's like checking your GPS after a road trip — "Did I go where I planned? Did it take longer? Did I use more gas?" Variances show the DIFFERENCE between what you PLANNED and what actually HAPPENED.

> 🧠 **Mnemonic: Favorable = "FEWER costs than expected" (good), Unfavorable = "UGH, costs were higher" (bad)**

### ✅ Direct Materials Variances

| Variance | Formula | ✅ Explanation |
|----------|---------|------|
| **Price Variance** | (Actual Price - Standard Price) × Actual Qty | Did we pay more or less per unit of material? |
| **Quantity/Usage Variance** | (Actual Qty - Standard Qty) × Standard Price | Did we use more or less material than planned? |

> 🧠 **Mnemonic: "PQ" = Price then Quantity** — Price uses Actual Qty, Quantity uses Standard Price

### ✅ Direct Labor Variances

| Variance | Formula | ✅ Explanation |
|----------|---------|------|
| **Rate Variance** | (Actual Rate - Standard Rate) × Actual Hours | Did we pay workers more or less per hour? |
| **Efficiency Variance** | (Actual Hours - Standard Hours) × Standard Rate | Did workers take more or less time than planned? |

> 🧠 **Mnemonic: "RE" = Rate then Efficiency** — same pattern as materials but for labor

### ✅ Overhead Variances

| Variance | ✅ Explanation |
|----------|------|
| **Spending Variance** | Did we spend more or less on overhead than expected? |
| **Efficiency Variance** | Did we use more or fewer allocation base hours? |
| **Volume Variance** | Did we produce more or fewer units than budgeted? (Fixed OH only) |

> 🧠 **Mnemonic: "SEV" = Spending, Efficiency, Volume**

### ✅ Flexible Budget Variance

```
Flexible Budget = Budgeted cost per unit × Actual units produced
```

> ✅ A flexible budget adjusts for actual volume — it answers "What SHOULD we have spent at the ACTUAL level of activity?"

## Module 3: Projection & Forecasting Techniques

### ✅ Forecasting Methods

> 🧠 **Mnemonic: "TREND"** — **T**ime series, **R**egression, **E**xponential smoothing, **N**aive approach, **D**elphi method

| Method | ✅ Explanation |
|--------|------|
| **Time Series** | Uses historical patterns to predict the future (like weather forecasting) |
| **Regression Analysis** | Finds the mathematical relationship between variables (y = a + bx) |
| **Exponential Smoothing** | Weighted average giving MORE weight to recent data |
| **Naive Approach** | Next period = This period (simplest possible forecast) |
| **Delphi Method** | Expert opinions collected anonymously in rounds until consensus |

### ✅ Regression Basics

```
y = a + bx
```
- **y** = dependent variable (what you're predicting — like sales)
- **a** = y-intercept (starting point)
- **b** = slope (how much y changes for each unit change in x)
- **x** = independent variable (what drives the prediction — like advertising spend)

> ✅ R² (Coefficient of Determination): Tells you how well the line fits the data. R² = 0.85 means 85% of changes in y are explained by x. Higher is better.

## Module 4: Budgeting

### ✅ What is a Master Budget?

Think of it as the company's "financial game plan" for the year. It starts with how much you'll SELL and works backward through everything else.

### ✅ Budget Flow

> 🧠 **Mnemonic: "Sales Produces Detailed Dollars Flowing Cash"**

```
Sales Budget (START HERE - the foundation!)
    ↓
Production Budget (How many units to make)
    ↓
├── Direct Materials Budget (How much raw stuff to buy)
├── Direct Labor Budget (How many worker hours needed)
└── Factory Overhead Budget (Other factory costs)
    ↓
Cost of Goods Sold Budget
    ↓
Selling & Administrative Budget
    ↓
Cash Budget
    ↓
Pro Forma Financial Statements (THE END PRODUCT)
```

### ✅ Production Budget Formula

```
Units to Produce = Budgeted Sales
                 + Desired Ending Inventory
                 - Beginning Inventory
```

> 🧠 **Mnemonic: "SEBA"** — **S**ales + **E**nding inventory - **B**eginning inventory = Amount to produce

### ✅ Types of Budgets

| Type | ✅ Explanation |
|------|------|
| **Static Budget** | Set at ONE activity level, doesn't change |
| **Flexible Budget** | Adjusts for actual activity level |
| **Zero-Based Budget** | Start from scratch every year — justify every dollar |
| **Kaizen Budget** | Built-in continuous improvement — costs decrease over time |
| **Rolling/Continuous Budget** | Always extends 12 months — add a new month as one drops off |
| **Activity-Based Budget** | Based on activities that drive costs, not just departments |

> 🧠 **Mnemonic: "SFZ KRA"** — **S**tatic, **F**lexible, **Z**ero-based, **K**aizen, **R**olling, **A**ctivity-based

## 🎯 Key Formulas Summary

| Formula | Expression |
|---------|------------|
| Materials Price Variance | (AP - SP) × AQ |
| Materials Quantity Variance | (AQ - SQ) × SP |
| Labor Rate Variance | (AR - SR) × AH |
| Labor Efficiency Variance | (AH - SH) × SR |
| Production Budget | Sales + Ending Inv - Beginning Inv |
| Flexible Budget | Standard cost/unit × Actual units |
| Breakeven Point | Fixed Costs / CM per unit |
| CM Ratio | (Sales - Variable Costs) / Sales |
